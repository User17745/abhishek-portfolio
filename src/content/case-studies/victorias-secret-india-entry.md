## Project Overview

Victoria's Secret, the world's largest lingerie brand, partnered with Apparel Group to enter the Indian market. As Consulting Project Manager, I led a 10-month implementation to bring the brand to millions of Indian consumers through Salesforce Commerce Cloud.

## The Challenge

Launching a global brand in India comes with unique complexities:

1. **Regulatory Compliance**: India-specific data localization and payment regulations
2. **Cultural Sensitivity**: Product presentation and marketing adaptation
3. **Infrastructure**: Integration with local payment gateways and logistics
4. **Consumer Behavior**: Understanding Indian shopping patterns and preferences

## Our Solution

### Platform Selection
Salesforce Commerce Cloud was chosen for its:
- Enterprise scalability
- Multi-brand capabilities
- Strong integration ecosystem
- Proven track record with global brands

### Implementation Approach

**Phase 1: Discovery & Design (Months 1-2)**
- Stakeholder workshops across US and India teams
- UX/UI design with cultural adaptation
- Technical architecture finalization

**Phase 2: Development (Months 3-7)**
- Core platform configuration
- Custom feature development
- Third-party integrations

**Phase 3: Testing & Optimization (Months 8-9)**
- Comprehensive UAT
- Performance optimization
- Load testing for launch traffic

**Phase 4: Launch & Stabilization (Month 10)**
- Phased rollout strategy
- 24/7 support during launch
- Post-launch optimization

## Key Integrations

- **Payments**: Razorpay, Paytm, Credit Cards, Net Banking
- **Logistics**: Delhivery, Ecom Express for pan-India delivery
- **Analytics**: Google Analytics 4, Adobe Analytics
- **CRM**: Salesforce Service Cloud integration

## Results

- Successful launch with zero critical issues
- 100K+ visitors on launch day
- Smooth checkout experience with 85% success rate
- Foundation laid for future store expansion

## Lessons Learned

- Early regulatory engagement is crucial for India
- Local partnerships accelerate go-to-market
- Consumer research should drive product decisions
- Cross-cultural communication requires extra attention
